02 // Pillar 2: Compliance, Trust & Transparency

Observed

On the live account dashboard screens where the active credit setup or limit is displayed, the app shows the borrowing numbers clearly but does not provide any direct button or link for the user to view, read, or download their official Sanction Letter or Loan Agreement.

Problem

This is a major RBI compliance gap and makes users feel legally unsafe. Under the RBI Digital Lending Guidelines, any app providing a credit facility must give the user 24/7, easy access to download their official signed loan contracts directly inside the user interface. If a user cannot see their actual legal paperwork, they suffer from "hidden terms anxiety"—worrying that the app might have fine-print traps about freezing their mutual funds. This lack of legal proof kills long-term trust, causing users to stop using the credit line.

Ship Instead

Add a clean, minimal option row directly inside the credit management card labeled "View Digital Loan Contract." Tapping this should open a simple screen where the user can view or instantly download their official, verified Sanction Letter and Key Fact Statement (KFS) as a clean PDF file. This keeps the app 100% compliant with the RBI and gives users complete legal peace of mind.

03 // Pillar 3: Engagement & Habit Retention Loop (The Squad Gullak)

Observed

On the live app's current savings screens, setting up an investment plan is a completely lonely, solo experience. If a user sets up a daily investment of ₹50 or ₹100, they do it by themselves. If they stop or pause their automated payments, it happens completely in secret within their own private account.

Problem

Lonely habits break easily. When young savers run a little short on cash at the end of the month, they simply pause their daily savings plan because nobody is watching and there is zero social consequence to quitting. This lack of external accountability is why the majority of retail savers stop their investments way too early, which hurts the app's long-term asset growth.

Ship Instead

Integrate your "Squad Gullak" Collective Milestone Dashboard. Let friends link up and save together for shared goals (like a vacation or a gadget). Put their daily progress bars side-by-side (tracking Pappu, Pandey, and You) with live consistency streaks. If the group budget falls behind, trigger a group-wide "Shortfall Trajectory Alert" with a simple button to fix it together (e.g., "Add +₹20 each to keep the trip alive"). Unlock rewards—like a discount on emergency cash interest—only when the entire squad keeps a joint 10-day saving streak active.

04 // Pillar 4: Conversion & Portfolio Pledging Optimization (The Micro-Pledger)

Observed

On the current emergency cash withdrawal and setup screen, the user is required to link and place a digital lock (lien) over their entire connected investment account before they can finalize the checkout.

Problem

This causes major "lock-in anxiety." If a user has accumulated ₹50,000 in savings and just needs a quick ₹3,000 cash advance to handle an unexpected utility bill, seeing that the app is locking down their whole life savings makes them incredibly defensive. They feel like they are losing total control over their money, panic, and abandon the checkout process at the very final step.

Ship Instead

Build a "Micro-Pledging Balance Slider" at the top of the withdrawal screen. Let the user select their required cash advance amount first (e.g., typing in ₹3,000). The UI will then dynamically show them that only a tiny, exact matching slice of their mutual funds is being locked to back that specific cash, while the remaining 94% of their savings pool stays completely open, free, and untouched.

05 // Pillar 5: Go-To-Market (GTM) & Distribution Strategy

Observed

The app is spending a major chunk of its growth budget on public digital ads, short Instagram finance videos, and hiring college student ambassadors to promote the app on university campuses.

Problem

This burns a massive amount of marketing cash on low-intent users. College students have very little steady income to save daily, and random users who click on social media ads drop out of the funnel the exact second the app asks them for serious banking or PAN details to link accounts, leading to a terrible return on ad spend.

Ship Instead

Stop running open public ads and pivot to a B2B2C model called a "Liquid Salary Account." Partner directly with corporate HR and payroll platforms (like Darwinbox or RazorpayX). Let working corporate employees check a simple box to auto-route a tiny slice of their daily salary into BlinkMoney. In return, they get an instant, pre-approved emergency cash reserve waiting right inside their corporate employee dashboard. This costs ₹0 in ad spend and acquires high-income, highly consistent savers.
